Our Process We’re Committed to Maintain Standard Work Process
Selection Selection of healthy explants from the bamboo mother plant.
Initiation Explants are surface-sterilised. ~10% contamination expected here.
Multiplication Successful cultures are multiplied by continuous subcultures (3-4 plants/subculture).
Rooting Cultures are placed in rooting medium. Minimal contamination (~1%).
Acclimatisation Rooted plantlets transfer to soil for adaptation. Overall mortality ~5% (or less).
Proper hardening and field plantation ensure final survival rates exceed 99%.
Green enenrgy takeoff High-Efficiency Biofuel and Power Source Bamboo biomass offers an energy value comparable to coal (4000 kcal/kg) but is a superior, sustainable option as it contains no sulfur. It can be continuously regenerated within short cultivation periods.
Green Electricity 1 MWh of power can be generated from 18,000 tonnes of biomass.
Bioethanol 1 kg of ethanol can be produced from 4 kg of bamboo.
CNG 1 kg of Compressed Natural Gas can be produced from 8 kg of bamboo biomass.
